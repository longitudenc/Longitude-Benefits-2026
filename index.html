Fundamental Care Limited Day Health Plan  ·  Effective June 1, 2026

Paycheck deductions change starting June 12th
Your new health plan deductions will appear on your June 12th paycheck — your first pay date of the new plan year. The amount deducted reflects your 2026 plan election. Questions about your deduction amount? Contact HR.
Your ID cards are on the way
Cards were mailed from Allied National and will arrive by USPS in a white envelope with a blue Allied National logo. Allow 5–7 business days — most cards will arrive June 5–10. PDF temporary ID cards will be emailed directly to you from HR as a bridge until your physical card arrives. Your coverage is active as of June 1st regardless. If you have a June 1st appointment, see the FAQ below.
Before your card arrives
Nothing to do yet
Your coverage is active as of June 1st — your card is on its way and there is nothing you need to do until it arrives. If you have an urgent appointment on or near June 1st, see the FAQ below for step-by-step guidance including what to do if you need to pay out of pocket and submit a claim.
When your card arrives — do these 4 things
1

Put the card in your wallet

Your member ID and the provider phone number are on the card. You will need the member ID to access Recuro Health and the Health Services HUB. Present the card at every medical appointment.

2

Register for Recuro Health — your free virtual doctor

Visit recurohealth.com and register using your member ID from your card. Free 24/7 access to board-certified physicians, therapists, and psychiatric providers by phone or video — no appointment needed, no copay.

3

Log in to your Health Services HUB

Go to healthserviceshub.com. Enter your member ID as the username. Password: benefits18. Do NOT click the red Sign Up button — use the green Log In button only.

4

Confirm your doctors are in-network

Call Patient Advocacy at 844-413-2499 to verify your doctors participate in the Prime Health Services network. Important: there are no restrictions on which hospital or facility you use — any hospital in the country is accessible on your plan.

Important — balance billing
Your only out-of-pocket cost on a covered service is your copay. If a provider sends a bill for more than your copay, do not pay it. After your claim is processed, Allied National will send you an Explanation of Benefits (EOB) showing exactly what you owe. If a bill exceeds your copay, call 866-332-1987 or email elite@alliednational.com — they will negotiate the bill on your behalf through Zelis at no cost to you.

Monday June 1st is the plan effective date, but no one will have a card yet and Allied National’s system may not have your records ready until Monday afternoon or Tuesday. Here is what to do:

1. Before your appointment: Call Allied National at 800-825-7531 when they open at 8am CST Monday. Explain that coverage started June 1st under the Fundamental Care Limited Day Health Plan and ask if your records are in the system yet. They may or may not be able to verify.

2. At your appointment: Tell the provider your plan is the Fundamental Care Limited Day Health Plan, administered by Allied National (800-825-7531), using the Prime Health Services physician network. Ask them to call Allied National directly for real-time verification. Request that they hold the claim rather than bill you immediately while verification is confirmed.

3. If the provider cannot verify and requires payment: You may need to pay out of pocket at the time of service. If this happens, pay only the amount due, get an itemized receipt, and keep all documentation.

4. Submitting a claim for reimbursement: Call Allied National Claims at 800-825-7531 or email claims@alliednational.com as soon as your records are confirmed in the system. Submit your itemized receipt and ask for reimbursement up to your plan’s covered amount for that service type. Your copay is your member responsibility — amounts above that on covered services should be reimbursed.

5. Follow up: Call Patient Advocacy at 844-413-2499 Monday afternoon or Tuesday — they will have full system access by then and can assist with any billing issues from the appointment.
